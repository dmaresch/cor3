Spink_junkee: Corporate shafts are way worse than refinery runs. Less debris more polished surfaces, you don't slide, you ricochet. If you want to move vertically in one you need to ride the counter rotation curve not the drop and never trust the first stabilization pulse.

A11_ta1k: what about sealed executive floors.? do stabilization conduits still connect those levels to utility access or are they isolated.?

Coolvac: Nothing is full isolated, redundancy is prioritized over security so theres almost always an old engineering route left in place. There's also usually unlocked safety exits inside the same as you see on any standard spin corridor.

RingRat07: Also watch the ventilation turbulence!!!!! High-tier offices dump climate waste downwards. There's a sideways push every few seconds that can save you if you time it right and snap your spine if you don't...

A11_ta1k: if someone had to hypothetically move fast after tripping an alarm would it make more sense to climb upward or drop towards utility decks.?

C0ldW4ke: Drop. Always drop. Security response usually funnels upwards. Downwards movement puts you into infrastructure chaos and there are usually more ways out at the lower floors

RingRat07: thin gravit also gives a safe window between correction pulses if you can manage that

Drift_stats: maintained and newer infrastructure is way more fun than the ruins.
