## CLASSIFIED OPERATION REPORT

CLASSIFIED

**Assessment ID:** CT1702, DTJC v3 couplings test - Extended

**Lead Investigator:** Simon Webb

**Date:** 2251.03.20

**Classification:** TOP SECRET - EYES ONLY

**Extended Report:**

"We have received a fresh batch of new couplings, the ones CORE Tech designed to be used in "unmanned bipedal units". Our task is to test them out on our existing models, before implementing them across upcoming product lines."

Although I have no reason to be cautious about any products that we receive from CORE, these couplings look somewhat different and too much like a prototype. We would have to run extensive tests on them, without analysing the composites themselves, which is prohibited under the terms of the license.

Also, the shipment container that those came in shows extensive wear and delivery information for one of the SOYUZ testing sites. Was that a mistake?"

### COMPLETE TEST RESULTS

- **Test 1 - Spin rate:** 15% increase in rotation speed without excessive degradation

- **Test 2 - Angular strike stress:** 7% increase in durability

- **Test 3 - Electrical compatibility:** Severe degradation in whole system operation. Reason unknown

- **Test 4 - Environmental resistance:** Extended resistance to corrosive environments

- **Test 5 - Magnetic properties:** Inconsistent results

- **Test 6 - Material scan (TOP SECRET):** *Traces of high energy particles "entombed" into the material, depleted uranium, nergalium-79 and several other unknown elements. Multiple scanning equipment failures.*

- **Test 7 - System Integration (TOP SECRET):** *Attempts to integrate couplings into the existing operational system of our BP-015 prototype drone have failed. Connecting to the couplings sensors corrupts the system within minutes. Incoming code overwhelmed it.*

**Investigator's Extended Analysis:**

"The results of these tests are remarkable, almost magical. I know it's not a scientific description but I have never seen a material like that. Its color, physical properties and the way it interacts with other parts … a mystery. We were not able to determine the magnetic properties of those couplings at all - impossible results, like that’s a material from a star."

These mystical properties lead to the next problem - compatibility. Drone "health" systems cannot properly determine the status of these couplings, therefore a "critical damage" status lights up and shuts the whole system down. Besides, our techs say that they affect the electrical grid as well, even if it's insulated.

All things considered, I am inclined to think that those couplings were never designed for use in any type of drone or bot at all. I am no expert but it's probably a stealth tech of some sort. I will contact the higher ups to process all that information and make some … decisions. Meanwhile all our personnel have been told to keep quiet about it. We do not want some international "incident" or SOYUZ troops storming our facility."

### ADDITION - TOP SECRET

*"The summary of all that information is that those couplings were modified by someone, likely SOYUZ "Razvedka" service, to prevent tempering with or any sort of analysis. The material itself is poisoned with radiation, making it unexaminable. Besides, there are traces of chemical tempering as well… who knows what it could be."*

*We believe that these couplings were delivered to us by mistake. Containers might have been switched during transit, at some point. The reason is unknown.*

*Our conclusion on the coupling themselves is that those are likely prototypes developed by some 3rd party, using CORE's DTJC technology. Likely an attempt at reverse engineering.*

*Protocol would dictate to either contact CIS or destroy the couplings entirely. Our complex has signal dampening which means that there is almost 0 chance that any signals got out. Besides, there is no trace that those couplings have any homing devices embedded.*

*All things considered, I would personally suggest getting rid of them entirely. Further study would require additional security measures with high risk of potential confrontation with 3rd parties. It's not my field of expertise, of course.*

*As for the technical part, I do not see this as a potential technology that would advance our designs. And, like I said, the material itself is too contaminated for further study.*
