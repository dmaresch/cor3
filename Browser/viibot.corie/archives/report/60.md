## ASSESSMENT CT1702.2 - CARGO MANIFEST

RESTRICTED

**Date:** 2251.03.15

**Source:** CORIE Intelligence Network

**Assessment ID:** CT1702.2

**Intelligence Report:**

"The cargo manifest is a mess. Half of it is in some kind of code, and the other half is in a language we can't identify. But we managed to piece together some information."

The shipment was supposed to contain standard coupling components, but what we received is anything but standard. The manifest lists several items that don't match what's actually in the containers.

Most concerning is the discrepancy in the delivery information. The manifest shows the shipment was supposed to go to a SOYUZ testing facility, not to us. This suggests either a mistake in routing or something more deliberate.

We also found references to some kind of "special handling" requirements that weren't included in our original order. This suggests the shipment was modified or redirected at some point during transit.

My assessment: this wasn't an accident. Someone wanted these couplings to end up in our hands, and they went to considerable trouble to make it happen. The question is why."
