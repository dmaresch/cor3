Supplement to the report on work in the cargo hold:

After clearing the container heap in the hold, the crew initiated diagnostics on the Fetcher 2 AMR logistics robot (Fetcher_242 unit) to assess the damage. Attempts were also made to restore its functionality.

Diagnostics showed that the damage were received:

Manipulators - minor damage, impact on functionality is minimal

Drive pods - Geometry is damaged, but functionality is preserved. Increased battery consumption is possible while use. Risk of drive overload while use. Replacement is recommended.

Body damage: a dent approximately 3 cm deep, according to the device's drawings, in the area of the battery pack. There is a high probability of battery damage. Disassembling the robot's body is not possible on the ship board.

With this damage the robot does not perform its function, giving an error P0A80.

Technician Charlie's attempts to restore the robot took considerable time and were unsuccessful. This failure may be due not only to the complexity of the situation but also to Charlie's own "complexities."

Robot Repair Assessment:

At best scenario - robot maintenance at a service center, replacement of battery cells or the entire battery

At worst scenario - acquisition of a new robot.
